Classic Jerk Chicken

A smoky, spicy Jamaican favorite - charred on the outside, juicy on the inside.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes (including marinating time)
  • Cook Time: 30 - 45 minutes
  • Total Time: 45 minutes
  • Servings: 4
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 2 tablespoons soysauce
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers, chopped (seeds removed for less heat)
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 ounce of lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the marinade by combining all marinade ingredients in a food processor or blender and blend until a smooth or slightly coarse paste forms. Taste and adjust for seasoning and spice level.
  2. Prepare the chicken by cleaning and cutting it into pieces. Pat the chicken pieces dry. For better flavor penetration, make a few deep slits in the meat with a knife.
  3. Marinate the chicken pieces in the jerk marinade by placing the chicken in a large bowl or a sealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over it. Massage the marinade into the chicken, ensuring it gets into all the slits and under the skin for at least 2 hours or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove chicken from marinade and shake off excess.
  5. Grill the chicken(This is the traditional method) turning occasionally, until cooked through and charred on the outside. Sear the chicken over direct heat to get some char, then move it to the cooler, indirect heat side to finish cooking slowly with the lid on, turning occasionally. Traditional methods use pimento wood to add smoke flavor. Cook until the internal temperature reaches 74°C.
  6. Let the chicken rest for 5-10 minutes before serving. Garnish with lime wedges and cilantro if desired. Jerk chicken is often served with sides like rice, coleslaw and peas or fried plantains.

Note: For an indoor alternative, you can bake the marinated chicken in a preheated oven at about 200°C for 35-45 minutes, until the juices run clear and the internal temperature reaches 74°C.
